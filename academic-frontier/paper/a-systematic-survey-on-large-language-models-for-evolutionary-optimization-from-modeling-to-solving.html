A Systematic Survey on Large Language Models for Evolutionary Optimization: From Modeling to Solving

Authors: Yisong Zhang, Ran Cheng, Guoxing Yi, Kay Chen Tan

ArXiv.org

DOI 10.48550/arxiv.2509.08269

Google ScholarOpenAlexSemantic Scholar (S2)

Reading file

Abstract: The survey organizes the use of large language models in evolutionary optimization into modeling and solving stages, then further separates solving into stand-alone optimizers, low-level algorithmic components, and high-level managerial roles. It is a useful evidence node for tracking how evolutionary optimization may connect with self-evolving agentic systems.

Why it matters

This survey is worth storing because it maps an emerging bridge between LLM reasoning and evolutionary optimization workflows. Even if it is not the central GPU-systems line, it is a useful frontier marker for where the group may influence agentic optimization research next.
